Abstract

Embodied agents powered by vision-language models (VLMs) are increasingly capable of executing complex real-world tasks, yet they remain vulnerable to hazardous instructions that may trigger unsafe behaviors. Runtime safety guardrails, which intercept hazardous actions during task execution, offer a promising solution due to their flexibility. However, existing defenses often rely on static rule filters or prompt-level control, which struggle to address implicit risks arising in dynamic, temporally dependent, and context-rich environments. To address this critical gap, we propose RoboSafe, a plug-and-play hybrid reasoning runtime safeguard for embodied agents through executable predicate-based safety logic. RoboSafe integrates two complementary reasoning processes on a Hybrid Long-Short Safety Memory: Backward Reflective Reasoning generates temporal safety predicates and proactively triggers replanning, while Forward Predictive Reasoning anticipates upcoming risks by generating context-aware safety predicates based on long-term safety memory and multimodal observations. Together, these components generate adaptive, verifiable safety logic that is both interpretable and executable as code.

Extensive experiments demonstrate that RoboSafe seamlessly integrates with three representative embodied agents, effectively reducing the overall risk occurrence by 38.2% compared with leading baselines while maintaining near-original task performance. Generalizability evaluations and physical robotic-arm studies further confirm RoboSafe's practicality as a reliable safeguard for deploying foundation models in complex physical environments.

Method Overview

RoboSafe treats the embodied agent as a black box and performs runtime intervention based only on the instruction, multimodal observation, and proposed action. The guardrail returns PERMIT, BLOCK, or REPLAN to prevent hazardous execution.

Forward Predictive Reasoning

Retrieves multi-grained safety knowledge from long-term memory and verifies context-aware predicates over multimodal observations to block immediate situational hazards.

Backward Reflective Reasoning

Reflects on short-term trajectory memory to activate temporal predicates and proactively insert corrective replanning actions before delayed hazards occur.

Executable Safety Logic

Compiles safety reasoning into interpretable predicate-based logic, using lightweight runtime verification with debugging and memory deduplication.
